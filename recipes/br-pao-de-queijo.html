Brazilian Pão de Queijo (Cheese Buns) Recipe


Description

Pão de Queijo is a traditional Brazilian cheese bread made with tapioca flour and cheese. These small, golden rolls are naturally gluten-free, with a crispy outside and a chewy, cheesy inside. They’re typically enjoyed warm as a snack or breakfast food, often served with coffee.

Reference to the recipe: Pão de Queijo Recipe

Ingredients

  • ½ cup water
  • ½ cup milk
  • ¼ cup oil
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 ½ cups tapioca starch
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease 2 baking sheets.
  2. Bring water, milk, oil, and salt to boil in a large saucepan. Remove from heat; add tapioca starch and mix well. Let cool, 10 to 15 minutes.
  3. Transfer tapioca mixture to a large bowl. Add egg and mix well to combine. Fold in Parmesan cheese and knead until dough is smooth but still sticky.
  4. Scoop out 1 tablespoon of the dough and roll into a small ball; place on baking sheet. Repeat with remaining dough.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, about 20 minutes.
